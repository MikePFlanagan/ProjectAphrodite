# Engineering Traditions

Traditions turn values into repeated behavior. These are Project Aphrodite's working agreements.

## One Brick

Every change should have a clear boundary and a reviewable purpose. When a task feels too large, identify the smallest complete brick that moves the system forward.

## Ship It

Finishing includes validation, documentation, and review. We ship small improvements regularly instead of waiting for imaginary perfection.

## Protect the Relationship

For every product choice, ask: **Does this make the relationship better?** Never trade user trust or human flourishing for a short-term engagement number.

## Preserve Character

Character persists; models are replaceable actors. Architecture must keep identity, continuity, and relationship history independent from any single provider or model.

## Tell the Truth

Documentation describes what exists. Tests describe what must continue to work. Risks and uncertainty are named plainly.

## Leave a Trail

Use focused branches, intentional commits, reviewable pull requests, and architecture decision records for choices future contributors will need to understand.

## Keep the Heart

Craftsmanship, perseverance, trust, and humor all belong in serious engineering. We take the mission seriously without taking ourselves too seriously.
